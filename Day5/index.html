About Jharkhand

Jharkhand, the "Land of Forests," is a state rich in natural beauty, mineral resources, and tribal culture. Located in eastern India, it boasts lush green forests, majestic waterfalls, and a vibrant cultural heritage that dates back thousands of years.

The state is home to numerous indigenous tribes, each with their unique traditions, festivals, and art forms. From the ancient Jagannath Temple in Ranchi to the breathtaking Hundru Falls, Jharkhand offers a diverse range of experiences for travelers seeking authentic cultural immersion and natural wonders.

Our mission at Jharkhand Trails is to showcase this incredible diversity while supporting local communities and promoting sustainable tourism practices that preserve the natural environment and cultural heritage for future generations.

Popular Destinations

Netarhat

Known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur," this hill station offers breathtaking sunrise and sunset views, dense forests, and a pleasant climate throughout the year.

Explore

Patratu Valley

Famous for its serpentine Patratu Valley road that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and the reservoir, it's a paradise for nature lovers and photographers.

Explore

Betla National Park

This national park is home to a variety of wildlife including tigers, elephants, and leopards. Visitors can enjoy jungle safaris and explore the ancient Betla Fort.

Eco Tourism

At Jharkhand Trails, we are committed to promoting sustainable tourism practices that minimize environmental impact while supporting local communities. Our eco-tourism initiatives include:

  • Tree plantation drives with tourist participation
  • Promotion of homestays to benefit local families directly
  • Wildlife conservation awareness programs
  • Plastic-free tourism initiatives
  • Guided nature walks with trained local guides

By choosing our eco-tourism packages, you contribute directly to the preservation of Jharkhand's natural beauty and the wellbeing of its tribal communities.
